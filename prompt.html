Barbara's %prompt command, and how to get the AI to do what you want:

Full Syntax:
%prompt <length of answer> <temperature> [prompt]

About the AI we use:
Barbara's AI is an autoregressive transformer, trained on The Pile: a diverse dataset of 800+ gigabytes of natural, unprocessed text from the internet. A transformer is a type of neural network that generates output one word at a time by predicting the next word, so if you give it the string "Hi my name is," it'll run it through the network and decide that a name should be the next word.

Temperature:
Temperature works like this: The AI knows millions of names, but the way it'll decide on just one name is based on how many times it shows up in the training data. For example, if the name John appears in the training data 10,000 times but Sophronia only appears 200 times, it'll choose John over Sophronia. This is great, but not if you're trying to get it to spit out a creative name. This is where temperature comes in. Temperature is just randomness, making the likelihood for Sophronia to be chosen closer to the likelihood of John being chosen. This means that instead of just using the most likely next word, Barbara will diversify. When writing creative text, or something where the most likely word isn't the best one, you'll want to use higher values for creativity. My recommendation is 0-0.2 for continuing with the most likely responses and 0.3-1.5 for more creative (though technically you can go up to 100 where almost every word has equal probability).

Zero/One/Multi-Shot Learning: Now we're talking about the actual text you give Barbara as a prompt. I think the best way to describe zero-shot, one-shot, and multi-shot learning is with some examples:

Zero-Shot Prompt:
Write the following passage in grammatically correct English:
Passage: me like doughnuts
Grammatical English:

One-Shot Prompt:
Write the following passage in grammatically correct English:
Passage: me like doughnuts
Grammatical English: I like doughnuts
Passage: friends with me will you be?
Grammatical English:

Multi-Shot Prompt:
Write the following passage in grammatically correct English:
Passage: me like doughnuts
Grammatical English: I like doughnuts
Passage: friends with me will you be?
Grammatical English: Will you be friends with me?
Passage: named-me is Jiano
Grammatical English: My name is Jiano
Passage: Is not you happy?
Grammatical English: Aren't you happy?
Passage: Me is very epic say you wouldn't?
Grammatical English:

In general, multi-shot is better solely because Barbara will understand what you want her to do better.

Another thing to note: For this, you would set the temperature to 0, because you don't want Barbara to be creative, you want her to accurately make the sentence gramatical
